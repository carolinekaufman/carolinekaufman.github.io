Academic Projects

Arith | COMP40 An image compressor and decompressor program written in C which operates on .ppm image files, compressing RGB pixel data into 32-bit words, and unpacking 32-bit words into RGB pixel data. Through this project, I studied bit-level representations and manipulations of data and floating-point arithmetic. Our final result compressor and decompressor both "produce less error than the reference implementation". Universal Machine | COMP40 (Current project) An emulator written in C for a Turing-complete Universal Machine.


Personal Projects

Ciralism Themes My tech story began like this: when I was in the 8th grade, I really loved One Direction. I ran a fan blog, and like most dedicated fan bloggers, I needed a perfect layout to display my posts. I couldn't find exactly what I wanted readily available, so I decided to make it, and taught myself HTML/CSS and some JavaScript. Over the next few years, I began designing, developing, and releasing themes for public use. I amassed a following of users in the thousands, and generated >$1k in revenue selling commissioned custom themes to individual bloggers.

Working on this project gave me a venue to learn about web development and design and to really dig into UX/UI. I still occasionally post new themes. It also taught me the valuable skill of self-studying and learning on the job. My work is available to view here (recent work is shown first).
