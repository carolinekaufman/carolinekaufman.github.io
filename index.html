Hi there, I'm

Caroline Kaufman

I'm a Computer Science student at Tufts University, and I'm passionate about technology, education, human-computer interaction, and all things French. I'm interested in interactions between technology and the people who use it, from natural language processing to UX/UI design, and especially in how we can leverage technology to make information and experiences accessible to everyone. I'm particularly excited by opportunities to use technology to make education accessible and delightful for all learners. I consider myself a people-person turned tech geek, and I love finding ways to combine my passions.

These days, I'm taking courses in Machine Structures and Assembly Language Programming, Discrete Math, Contemporary Challenges in French Culture, and Linguistics of Second Language Acquisition. I'm also working as a teaching assistant for the Intro to CS course at Tufts, leading a team of fantastic health educators for the Peer Health Exchange, and facilitating weekend coding workshops to give local students exposure to CS with Tufts Girls of Code. If I'm not up to my ears in assembly code or pseudocoding on whiteboards with my COMP11 students, you can probably find me teaching workshops on communication and consent in high schools around Boston, poring over design articles on Medium, or curled up in the library with a French novel.
